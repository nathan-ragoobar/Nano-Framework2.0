\begin{DoxyPrecond}{Precondition}

\end{DoxyPrecond}
Column $ k $ of the returned matrix is an eigenvector corresponding to eigenvalue number $ k $ as returned by \doxylink{classEigen_1_1ArpackGeneralizedSelfAdjointEigenSolver_a17962f248d5d9b58d1b49ba74c4fda15}{eigenvalues()}. The eigenvectors are normalized to have (Euclidean) norm equal to one. If this object was used to solve the eigenproblem for the selfadjoint matrix $ A $, then the matrix returned by this function is the matrix $ V $ in the eigendecomposition $ A V = D V $. For the generalized eigenproblem, the matrix returned is the solution $ A V = D B V $
